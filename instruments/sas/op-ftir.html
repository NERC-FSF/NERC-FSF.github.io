    Overview

    The Midac Model M4401-SP Open Path Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) field spectrometer is a portable FTIR instrument providing hyperspectral measurements in the 1.5 to 15.5 μm spectral range. It is ideally suited for providing point measurements in support of atmospheric sciences, wildfire ecology, or volcanology. In particular, the lightweight and portable nature of the instrument, coupled with high spectral resolution and broad spectral range, has made it a critical and key instrument used by the UK volcanology community, which often must plan rapidly around unpredictable eruptions in often difficult to reach areas.

    Technical Specification

    Value
    Spectral Range 5000 - 700 cm-1
    Optical Resolution programmable from 0.5 - 32cm-1
    Detector type Sterling Pump Cooled MCT
    Beam Splitter ZnSe
    Power Source 12 V
    Weight 7.5 kg
    Size 36 × 18 × 17 cm

    Accessories

    • Open Path Input Optics -10” Φ Newtonian telescope
    • 3” Φ Newtonian telescope, 10 millirad (mrad) Field Sources
    • 20” diameter collimated beam form IR glower within a 20” parabolic gold reflector
    • Field portable IR-301 blackbody sources with attachment for connection to FTIR

    Example Applications

    • Atmospheric measurements of carbon dioxide (CO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2) and methane (CH4)
    • Volcanology
    • Measurements of wildfires
    • Biosphere-atmosphere intractions
    • Reflectance measurements of materials in the infrared region
    • Geological surveying
